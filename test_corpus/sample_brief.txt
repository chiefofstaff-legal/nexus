APPLICANT'S HEADS OF ARGUMENT

1. INTRODUCTION

1.1 This application concerns the unlawful termination of Mining Right MR-2024-0789 granted to the Applicant in terms of Section 23 of the Mineral and Petroleum Resources Development Act 28 of 2002 ("the MPRDA").

1.2 The Applicant seeks an order:
    (a) Declaring the termination of Mining Right MR-2024-0789 unlawful and void;
    (b) Reinstating the said Mining Right with immediate effect;
    (c) Ordering the First Respondent to pay damages in the amount of R45,000,000.00;
    (d) Costs of suit on an attorney and client scale.

2. FACTUAL BACKGROUND

2.1 The Applicant was granted Mining Right MR-2024-0789 on 15 June 2024, authorising platinum group metals extraction in the Bushveld Complex, Limpopo Province.

2.2 On 10 December 2025, the First Respondent, through its subsidiary Bushveld Operations (Pty) Ltd, commenced mining operations on the same area without authorisation, in direct violation of the Applicant's exclusive mining rights.

2.3 The Second Respondent, despite being notified of the encroachment on 15 December 2025, failed to take any enforcement action and subsequently purported to "cancel" the Applicant's Mining Right on 20 January 2026.

3. LEGAL SUBMISSIONS

3.1 Section 23(1) of the MPRDA provides that a mining right grants the holder the exclusive right to mine minerals on the land to which such right relates.

3.2 The principle in Bengwenyama Minerals (Pty) Ltd v Genorah Resources (Pty) Ltd 2011 (4) SA 113 (CC) confirms that mining rights holders are entitled to the protection of their constitutional property rights under Section 25 of the Constitution.

3.3 The cancellation of the Applicant's Mining Right without following the procedures prescribed in Section 47 of the MPRDA is procedurally unfair and substantively irrational.

4. RELIEF SOUGHT

The Applicant respectfully submits that this Honourable Court should grant the relief as set out in the Notice of Motion.
